Experimental Method

Materials

- PTFE (polytetrafluoroethylene) target
- Nanosecond pulsed laser (10–100 mJ)
- Vacuum chamber
- Target holder
- Optical focusing system
- Thrust measurement system
- Data acquisition system

Experimental Setup

The PTFE target will be mounted inside a vacuum chamber. A nanosecond pulsed laser will be focused onto the target surface through an optical system. Laser pulses will ablate the PTFE surface, producing a plasma plume. The generated thrust will be measured together with propellant mass loss and plasma characteristics.

Test Parameters

- Laser pulse energy: 10–100 mJ
- Pulse duration: Nanoseconds
- Vacuum pressure: High vacuum
- Propellant: PTFE

Measurements

The experiment will record:

- Thrust
- Impulse per pulse
- Specific impulse (Isp)
- PTFE mass loss
- Plasma plume direction
- Repeatability of measurements

Data Analysis

Experimental data will be compared with theoretical laser-ablation models to evaluate propulsion performance and determine the feasibility of the concept for future CubeSat missions.
